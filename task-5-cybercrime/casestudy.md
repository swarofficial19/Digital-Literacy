Part A: Case Study 

Topic: UPI QR Code Scam


Description: UPI payment fraud is a sophisticated cybercrime where attackers exploit the "Request Money" feature of UPI apps to deceive victims.

How it happens (Step-by-Step):


The Bait: An attacker poses as a buyer on a platform like OLX or Facebook Marketplace and expresses interest in an item the victim is selling.


The Deception: The attacker claims they are sending money and sends a QR code via WhatsApp, instructing the victim to "scan it to receive the payment".

The Trap: When the victim scans the code, they are actually authorizing a "Request Money" transaction rather than receiving funds.

The Theft: The victim enters their UPI PIN, thinking they are confirming the receipt of money. Instead, the PIN authorizes the transfer of funds from the victim's account to the attacker's.


Target Group: This crime typically targets college students, home-sellers, and small business owners who are frequent users of digital payment apps.

Consequences: Victims face immediate financial loss, often ranging from small amounts to their entire bank balance. Beyond the monetary loss, it leads to a loss of trust in digital banking systems and significant stress.
