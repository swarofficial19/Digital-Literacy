A common situation where poor digital communication causes significant issues is during group project coordination via instant messaging. In a hypothetical scenario, a student sent a blunt, one-word message—"Redo"—to a teammate regarding their contribution. Without the context of a professional greeting or specific feedback, the teammate interpreted the tone as hostile and demotivating. This led to a breakdown in collaboration, a delayed submission, and a strained working relationship.

To prevent this, the student should have applied basic email etiquette to the chat environment. Instead of a blunt command, they could have used a structured approach: starting with a professional greeting, providing specific reasons why the work needed revision, and offering to discuss the changes over a brief call. Clear, empathetic communication ensures that the focus remains on the quality of the task rather than personal conflict.
